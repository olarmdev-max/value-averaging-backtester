### Part 2: The Corner Cases (What is MISSING)
To actually build this algorithm, a quant developer would need answers to several critical edge cases and mathematical boundaries that the video completely ignores.

**1. The "100% Utilization" Corner Case (Running out of cash)**
*   *The Scenario:* A prolonged bear market causes the algorithm to execute Rule 3 (Incremental Buy) every day until cash drops to $0.00. The portfolio is 100% utilized, but the market keeps dropping.
*   *Missing Info:* Does the algorithm simply do nothing and wait? Or does it have a "rebalancing" rule where it sells off other, better-performing allocations to free up cash to buy the deep dip?

**2. The Dynamic Sizing Formula (How big is the buy?)**
*   *The Scenario:* The market drops 1% on Monday, and 10% on Tuesday.
*   *Missing Info:* Does the algorithm buy the exact same dollar amount on both days? Or does the algorithm dynamically size its purchases based on the severity of the dip? We know the "Aggressiveness" hyperparameter (1 through 4) changes the speed of deployment (represented by "Buys Left" on the dashboard), but the exact formula for Buy_Size = f(Cash, Aggressiveness, Dip_Severity) is hidden.

**3. The "Wash Sale" Corner Case (Tax Implications)**

Does it have logic to prevent selling at a loss just to capture a daily target? The video only mentions taxes regarding profits, ignoring the mechanics of losses.

**4. Extreme Gap-Ups / Gap-Downs**
*   *The Scenario:* A fund closes at $100. Overnight, extreme news causes the fund to open at $50 (a 50% gap down).
*   *Missing Info:* Does the algorithm blow all its cash at once to "catch up" to its Value Averaging path? Or is it strictly limited to one predefined incremental buy per day, regardless of how massive the gap is?
